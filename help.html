What is this CR thing?

CR is tech's new policy regarding transcript grades. When your semester 2 report card comes this year, you may
choose to convert the grade into pass fail instead of having a numerical grade. This pass/fail can be used
on any subject you want (ie. I want to CR my APWH grade, but leave my chem grade).

CRs are only applied to the grades THIS SEMESTER. When a grade has been CRed, the numerical value is not
calculated into your final transcript average, and the credits will not be used to calculate GPA, although
you will still have that count towards graduation.

If you want an example of a CRed grade, look at lab.


Conservative Estimate

This option is for the people with relatively few new grades to work with, and are afraid that their average
will drop in the next few semesters. The conservative estimate gives you a 3 point range. If your grade is within
3 points of the average, the CR tool will not suggest the item for CR to allow you to build more credits in your
transcript.

ie. A freshie has a total of 12 credits. Freshie wants to CR 4 of his classes this semester.
However, freshie gets afraid he only has 8 credits to fumble with, and he might not do well Sophmore Year.
So, freshie wants more credits to keep himself afloat if that happens so his grade doesn't hit rock bottom
while taking preCalc. Conservative estimate reccomends him to only CR 2 of his classes for best results.
